Frequently Asked Questions

Saturn is roughly 72 light minutes away or average. Saturn is a planet within our solar system that orbits the sun. The Kuiper Belt is only about 13 light hours in diameter, so things in the solar system uses light minutes or hours.

As of June 8, 2023: 146 moons. But, only 7 are every large/bright enough to be visible through an amature telescope on Earth.

Saturn has a diameter of about 75,000 miles, making it about 9 times the diameter of Earth. If Earth is a nickle, Saturn is a Volleyball. About 764 Earth can fit within Saturn.
