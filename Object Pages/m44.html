M44 - "Beehive Cluster" - NGC 2632

Location

DEC = +19° 59' 00"
Distance = 577.3 ly

Visibility

Constellation = Cancer
Months = December - June
Moonlight = Any

Guest Viewing

Eyepiece = 30mm 3"
Guest Ability = Easy
Appearence = Bright, blue-white stars (points of light).

Frequently Asked Questions

Astronomy is very interprative, so what one person sees may not be what you see. The "Beehive" shape is more visible when you zoom out to see the whole cluster, with the brighter stars appearing to make the shape of a beehive. If you cannot see the shape, that is normal and okay!

The stars within the cluster are around 625.4 million years old (600-700 million year old range).

There are over 1000 stars that make up the entire cluster!
