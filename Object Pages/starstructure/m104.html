M04 - "Sombrero Galaxy" - NGC 5272

Location

R.A. = 12h 39m 59.4s
DEC = -11° 37' 23"
Distance = 28 million ly

Visibility

Constellation = Virgo
Months = March - August
Moonlight = Less than 1/2 Moon

Guest Viewing

Eyepiece = 17mm
Guest Ability = Medium - Hard
Appearence = Semi-faint, fuzzy line with a small ball in the middle of the line representing the core of the galaxy. Under dark skies and good eyes, a dark line (dust lane) can be visible across the smudge.

Frequently Asked Questions

It is a spiral galaxy, like the MW. It gives off elements of both spirals and elliptical galaxies, but currently we believe it is an edge-on spiral galaxy.

It has several hundred billion stars, or roughly the same as the MW. Its diameter is about 60,000 ly across, or about 60% the MW.

That's a dust lane! That is gas, dirt, rock, and well...dust, that is clumped in a very defined loop around the outside of the galaxy. It blocks the light from behind it, giving it the dark appearence.
